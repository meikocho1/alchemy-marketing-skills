# Cultural Context — perception is coded by where you are

Psycho-logic works on *perceived* reality, and perception is written in a local language you don't automatically speak. A guarantee that reads as "confident" in one market reads as "desperate — why would they promise that?" in another. Cheapness signals "smart value" here and "they cut corners, how disrespectful" there. Before you reframe, signal, or redesign a moment, establish the local rules for what the move will *mean*. This is the lens; **market-recon** is the fieldwork.

## The principle

Sutherland's levers all act on perception, and perception is set by culture — so **the same lever, unchanged, produces different behaviour in different markets.** The default failure is exporting a home-market playbook and assuming the psychology transfers. It doesn't. What transfers is the *method* (find the perception, not the metric); the *content* of "premium," "trust," "safe," and "expensive" is local and must be re-derived per market.

## The dimensions that rewire the moves

Treat these as hypotheses about the target market — dials, not verdicts. For each move you're about to make, ask which dials bend it.

- **Uncertainty avoidance** — how much reassurance, detail, guarantee, and precedent people need before acting. High-avoidance markets reward explicit guarantees, credentials, and "everyone else does this too"; low-avoidance ones find the same reassurance patronising. *(bends costly-signaling, experience-design)*
- **Individualism vs collectivism** — does "what you gain" persuade, or "what your family/group/neighbours will think"? Social proof and belonging beat personal-benefit copy in collectivist markets. *(bends reframing, costly-signaling)*
- **Status & power distance** — whether visible hierarchy, prestige, and authority *attract* or *repel*. Status signals that lift desire in one market read as vulgar or untrustworthy in another. *(bends reframing, costly-signaling)*
- **High- vs low-context communication** — how explicit the message must be. Low-context markets (e.g. much of the US) want it spelled out; high-context ones (e.g. Japan) read tone, restraint, and what's *left unsaid* — over-explaining signals mistrust. *(bends every message and reframe)*
- **Mental-accounting & price norms** — what "expensive" means, whether round or .99 prices signal quality, whether haggling is expected, which pocket a cost lands in. Price framing is not portable. *(bends reframing)*
- **Trust locus** — where credibility comes from: institutions and certifications, personal relationships and introductions, or peer reviews and ratings. Aim your costly signal at the local source of trust. *(bends costly-signaling)*
- **Taboo & sacredness** — what must not be joked about, discounted, rushed, or made "fun." Cross this and no clever frame survives it. Critical in death, health, money, religion, and family. *(vetoes moves that violate it)*

## How to apply

1. **Define the market precisely.** Not "Japan" but "urban Kanto seniors" or "rural Kansai families." Country is the coarsest unit; region-within-country, urban/rural, generation, and subculture often matter more.
2. **List the moves you were about to make** (the reframe, the signal, the felt-moment fix from the other skills).
3. **Run each move through the dials.** For every move ask: in *this* market, does it still mean what I intend? Which dimension flips it? Rewrite the move to the local reading, or drop it.
4. **Get the live data, don't reason from stereotype.** These dimensions generate *hypotheses*; a stereotype applied confidently is a trap. Send the specifics to **market-recon** to check against real local players, and to **counterintuitive-tests** to validate cheaply before rollout.
5. **Never let a taboo be "optimised."** If a move crosses a local sacredness line, no efficiency gain is worth it — cut it first.

## 日本語の使用例

- 「US で当たった訴求をそのまま日本で出したい」→ 心理は輸出できない。**高コンテクスト＋高不確実性回避**の日本では、はっきり言い切る強い断定コピーや過剰な説明が「押しつけがましい/信用できない」と読まれる。手法は同じでも中身（"高級""安心"の意味）は市場ごとに作り直す。
- 「値下げで攻めれば地方でも勝てるはず」→ **価格の意味は非携帯**。地域や世代によって "安い＝手抜き/失礼" と読まれる。安さを出す前に、その市場での価格の読まれ方を確認。
- 「レビュー星の数を前面に出す」→ **信頼の所在**が市場で違う。紹介・地域の評判・寺や医師の推薦が効く層に、他人のレビューは刺さらないことがある。シグナルは"その土地の信頼の源"に向ける。
- 葬儀/シニア領域：**タブー/神聖さ**が最優先の制約。割引・カジュアル化・スピード訴求は文脈次第で不謹慎に転ぶ。さらに関東/関西で葬送慣習（骨上げ・通夜の位置づけ等）が異なるため、「日本」ではなく地域単位で知覚ルールを立ててから reframing / costly-signaling / experience-design に渡す。
